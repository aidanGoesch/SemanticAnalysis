A living mountain appeared alongside ancient underwater creatures and a bright sky.
Small ancient creatures moved around as the mountain blinked and eventually fell asleep.
While it dreamed, time accelerated and landscapes rapidly transformed.
Dinosaurs ran past as the mountain’s body changed shape.
Ice and snow covered the land as glaciers pushed the mountain into a boulder.
The climate warmed, forests grew, and the boulder rested beneath a tree.
Human activity awakened the boulder as men chopped wood and built nearby homes.
Disturbed, the boulder uprooted itself and wandered until it was captured by people.
The boulder was carved, reshaped, and put to work as machinery.
Time sped forward as it became a millstone and then a wheel.
Soldiers seized it, crushed it down, and fired it as a cannonball.
The shattered stone sank into the sea and slowly washed ashore.
Now small, the rock moved through a modern world of farms and roads.
Scientists discovered it and placed it in a museum.
An accident sent it tumbling into the streets, where it was broken apart again.
Reduced to dust, it was swept away and transformed into glass.
That glass became part of a telescope launched into space.
The spacecraft collided with a planet and melted into the surface.
Over time, a new mountain formed on that planet and fell asleep once more.
